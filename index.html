Elementary Stochastic Processes

ELEMENTARY STOCHASTIC CALCULUS — THE COIN-TOSSING EXPERIMENT

Toss a fair coin n times over a total time horizon T. Heads you win, tails you lose. In the basic game the stake is ±$1 per toss; in the refined game it is ±√(T/n), so that the variance of the final winnings stays equal to T no matter how many steps you take. As n → ∞, the path of Si approaches Brownian motion.
